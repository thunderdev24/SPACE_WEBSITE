    The Darkest Mysteries of the Universe

    At the center of every black hole lies a singularity, a point where density becomes infinite and the laws of physics as we know them break down. Surrounding it is the event horizon, the invisible boundary beyond which nothing can return.

    Scientists believe that supermassive black holes exist at the center of almost every galaxy, including our own Milky Way. These cosmic giants can be millions or even billions of times more massive than the Sun.

    Despite their terrifying reputation, black holes are essential for understanding the evolution of galaxies, space-time, and the universe itself.
